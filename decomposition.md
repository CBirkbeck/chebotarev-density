## R1 — `primeIdealZetaSum_univ_tendsto_log` (`Density.lean`)

**Goal:** `Σ_𝔭 N𝔭^{-s} ~ log(1/(s-1))` as `s ↓ 1`.

### Source — Sharifi 7.1.12 proof (p. 140)

> "PROOF. We sketch part of the proof. Again, we consider the logarithm
> of the Euler product, noting that
>   log ζ_K(s) ~ Σ_𝔭 N𝔭^{-s},
> and the latter sum is at most `[K:ℚ] Σ_p p^{-s} ~ [K:ℚ] log(s-1)^{-1}`,
> from which we obtain convergence of the Euler product on Z(1) to an
> analytic function. We can then compare the Euler product over a finite
> sum of primes with the partial sum in the Dirichlet series over ideals
> divisible only by those primes to obtain equality in said region. We
> omit the argument regarding its meromorphic continuation and simple
> pole."

### Sub-lemmas

- **L1** `primeIdealZetaHigherTail_bounded` — the `k ≥ 2` tail of
  `log(1 - x)⁻¹ = Σ x^k/k` is bounded. Source: implicit in Sharifi's
  `~` (Notation 7.1.10, p. 140: "f ~ g if they differ by a function
  analytic at 1").

- **L2** `primeIdealZetaSum_le_finrank_smul_riemannPrimeSum` —
  `Σ_𝔭 N𝔭^{-s} ≤ [K:ℚ] Σ_p p^{-s}`. Source quote: "the latter sum is
  at most `[K:ℚ] Σ_p p^{-s}`".

- **L3** `riemannPrimeSum_asymp_log` — case `K = ℚ` of the main
  theorem; analytic input. Discharge: simple pole of `ζ` at `s = 1`
  (mathlib `riemannZeta_residue_one`) + Euler product.

- **L4** `primeIdealZetaSum_ge_log_minus_bounded` — lower bound from
  simple pole of `ζ_K`. Discharge: mathlib
  `NumberField.tendsto_sub_one_mul_dedekindZeta_nhdsGT`.

- **R1** = composition: upper bound (L2 + L3) and lower bound (L4)
  sandwich `primeIdealZetaSum` between `log(1/(s-1)) + O(1)` and
  `[K:ℚ] log(1/(s-1)) + O(1)`; the constant sharpens to `1` via the
  simple-pole equality.

## R2 — `exists_unique_frobeniusAt` (`Frobenius.lean`)

**Goal:** for unramified prime `𝔓` of `𝓞 L` over `𝔭`, ∃! σ ∈ D_𝔓 acting
as `(N𝔭)`-power Frobenius on `𝓞 L / 𝔓`.

### Source

Sharifi §7.1–7.2 *uses* this as a black box; the proof lives in §2.6
and standard Lang ANT VII §2 Theorem 2.

### Sub-lemmas

- **L1** mathlib `IsFractionRing.stabilizerHom_surjective` — `D_𝔓 →*
  Gal(κ(𝔓)/κ(𝔭))` is surjective. Sharifi §2.6.

- **L2** mathlib `Ideal.Quotient.stabilizerHom_ker` — kernel of L1 =
  `inertiaGroup`.

- **L3** NEW `inertiaGroup_trivial_of_unramified` —
  `inertiaGroup K L 𝔓 = ⊥` when 𝔓 unramified. Discharge: mathlib
  `Ideal.ramificationIdx_eq_one_of_isUnramifiedAt` + the identity
  `|I_𝔓| = e(𝔓|𝔭)`.

- **L4** mathlib `FiniteField.frobeniusAlgEquivOfAlgebraic` — residue
  Galois group cyclic, generated by `x ↦ x^{N𝔭}`.

- **R2** = composition: L3 ⟹ L1 + L2 give iso `D_𝔓 ≅ Gal(κ(𝔓)/κ(𝔭))`;
  L4 gives unique preimage of the absolute Frobenius.

## R3 — `exists_dedekindZeta_factorisation` (`ZetaProduct.lean`)

**Goal:** for `L/K` abelian, `∃ L_χ : ζ_L = ∏_χ L_χ`, `L_1 = ζ_K`,
`L_χ(1) ≠ 0` for `χ ≠ 1`.

### Source

Sharifi 7.1.16 (factorisation), 7.1.18 (abelian Euler product),
7.1.19 (analytic extension + non-vanishing).

### Sub-lemmas

- **L1** `exists_artinLSeries_eulerProduct_abelian` — Euler product on
  `Re s > 1`. Source verbatim (7.1.18, p. 141):
  > "For a Galois extension L/K and an abelian character
  > χ: Gal(L/K) → ℂ^×, then L(χ,s) = ∏_𝔭 (1-χ(𝔭) N𝔭^{-s})^{-1} =
  > Σ_𝔞 χ(𝔞) N𝔞^{-s} for Re(s) > 1."

- **L2** `dedekindZeta_local_factor_eq_product_artin_local` — local
  factor identity at each prime. Source: Sharifi 7.1.16 specialised
  per-prime.

- **L3** `character_sum_geometry_of_numbers_bound` — Sharifi 7.1.19
  step 1a (p. 142, verbatim):
  > "The geometry of numbers can be used to show that the number of
  > ideals 𝔞 of 𝓞_K with N𝔞 ≤ N for N ≥ 1 and χ(𝔞) = ζ is CN +
  > O(N^{1-d^{-1}}), where C is a constant independent of ζ. … We
  > note that Σ_{N𝔞≤N} χ(𝔞) = O(N^{1-d^{-1}})."

- **L4** `artinLSeries_analytic_extension` — Sharifi 7.1.19 step 1b
  (p. 142, verbatim):
  > "By Lemma 7.1.5, we therefore have that Σ_𝔞⊂𝓞_K χ(𝔞) N𝔞^{-s}
  > converges absolutely and uniformly on every compact subset of
  > Z(1 - d^{-1})."
  Discharge: mathlib's `LSeries` convergence machinery applied to L3's
  bound.

- **L5** `artinLSeries_one_ne_zero` — Sharifi 7.1.19 step 2 (p. 142,
  verbatim):
  > "For the nonvanishing, write log ζ_L(t) = Σ_χ log L(χ,t) and
  > observe that, up to a bounded function as t → 1+, the latter sum
  > has absolute value at least (1 - Σ_χ m_χ) log(t-1)^{-1}, where m_χ
  > is the order of vanishing of L(χ,s) at s = 1. But if some m_χ ≥
  > 1, then 1 - Σ_χ m_χ ≤ 0, which is impossible since log ζ_L(s) ~
  > log(s-1)^{-1}."

- **R3** = composition.

## R4 — `chebotarev_cyclotomic` (`Cyclotomic.lean`)

**Goal:** for `L = K(μ_m)` cyclotomic and `σ ∈ Gal(L/K)`,
`δ({𝔭 : σ_𝔭 = σ}) = 1/|G|`.

### Source — Sharifi 7.2.1 proof (pp. 142–143)

> "[…] φ_𝔭(ζ_m) = ζ_m^{N𝔭} for a primitive mth root of unity ζ_m,
> and therefore χ(φ_𝔭) depends only on N𝔭 modulo m.
>
> Much as before, we have
>   log L(χ,s) ~ Σ_𝔭 χ(𝔭) N𝔭^{-s}
> for Re(s) > 1. Given σ ∈ Gal(F(μ_m)/F) with σ(ζ_m) = ζ_m^a for some a
> prime to m, we have
>   Σ_χ χ(σ)χ(𝔭)^{-1} = { 0 if a ≢ N𝔭 mod m, |G| otherwise.
>
> Now, on the one hand we have Σ_χ χ(σ)^{-1} log L(χ,s) ~ |G|
> Σ_{N𝔭≡a mod m} N𝔭^{-s}, whereas on the other we have Σ_χ χ(σ)^{-1}
> log L(χ,s) ~ log ζ_K(s) ~ log(s-1)^{-1}, since we know that L(χ,1)
> ≠ 0 for all nontrivial χ. Comparing the two equations, we obtain
> that the Dirichlet density of 𝔭 with φ_𝔭 = σ is 1/|G|."

### Sub-lemmas

- **L1** `cyclotomic_frobenius_acts_as_norm_power` — verbatim:
  "we have φ_𝔭(ζ_m) = ζ_m^{N𝔭} for a primitive mth root of unity ζ_m".

- **L2** `log_artinLSeries_asymp_character_sum` — verbatim:
  "Much as before, we have log L(χ,s) ~ Σ_𝔭 χ(𝔭) N𝔭^{-s}".

- **L3a** `character_orthogonality_cyclotomic_eq` (matching case) —
  verbatim: "Σ_χ χ(σ)χ(𝔭)^{-1} = … |G| if a ≡ N𝔭 mod m".

- **L3b** `character_orthogonality_cyclotomic_ne` (vanishing case) —
  verbatim: "Σ_χ χ(σ)χ(𝔭)^{-1} = 0 if a ≢ N𝔭 mod m".

- **L4** `cyclotomic_density_from_two_sided_asymp` — verbatim:
  "Comparing the two equations, we obtain that the Dirichlet density
  of 𝔭 with φ_𝔭 = σ is 1/|G|".

- **R4** = composition.

## R6 — `chebotarev_density` (`Main.lean`)

**Goal:** for general Galois `L/K` and conjugacy class `C ⊆ Gal(L/K)`,
`δ({𝔭 : σ_𝔭 ∈ C}) = |C|/|G|`.

### Source — Sharifi 7.2.2 Step 1 (p. 143)

(Quoted in full in the file docstring.)

### Sub-lemmas

- **L1** `count_primes_above_with_frobenius_eq_sigma` — verbatim:
  "exactly |G|/f|C| of these have Frobenius σ".

## Sharifi notation slips

Two cosmetic Sharifi notation slips noted during the re-read; the Lean
statements use the mathematically correct form:

- p. 143: "E = K^⟨σ⟩" should be "E = L^⟨σ⟩".
- p. 143: "P has degree one over F" should be "over K" (F isn't
  introduced).
