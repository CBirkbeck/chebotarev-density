1 Chebotarev: abelian case via cyclotomic crossing

The abelian case is reduced to the cyclotomic case by Chebotarev’s original crossing trick. Source: Sharifi 7.2.2 Step 2 (pp. 143–144).

Lemma 1.1
#

Lean declarations

  • Chebotarev.cyclic_subgroup_meets_G_times_one_trivially

Let \(G, H\) be finite groups, \(\sigma \in G\), \(\tau \in H\). If \(\left\lvert G \right\rvert \mid \mathrm{ord}(\tau )\), then \(\left\langle (\sigma ,\tau ) \right\rangle \cap (G\times \{ 1\} ) = \{ 1\} \).

Proof ▶

Pure group theory. If \((\sigma ^k, \tau ^k) \in G\times \{ 1\} \) then \(\tau ^k = 1\), so \(\mathrm{ord}(\tau )\mid k\), hence \(\left\lvert G \right\rvert \mid k\), hence \(\sigma ^k = 1\).

Lemma 1.2

Lean declarations

  • Chebotarev.liminf_density_S_sigma_ge_card_H_n_div_GH

Let \(L/K\) be a finite abelian Galois extension with \(G=\mathrm{Gal}(L/K)\), \(\sigma \in G\), and \(m\ge 1\) coprime to the discriminant of \(L\), so that \(\mathrm{Gal}(L(\zeta _m)/K)\cong G\times H\) with \(H=\mathrm{Gal}(K(\zeta _m)/K)\subseteq (\mathbb {Z}/m\mathbb {Z})^\times \). Set \(H_n = \left\{ \tau \in H \; \middle |\; \left\lvert G \right\rvert \mid \mathrm{ord}(\tau ) \right\} \). Then

\[ \delta _{\mathrm{inf}}\bigl(\left\{ \mathfrak {p}\subset \mathcal{O}_K \; \middle |\; \sigma _\mathfrak {p}= \sigma \right\} \bigr) \; \ge \; \frac{\left\lvert H_n \right\rvert }{\left\lvert G \right\rvert \cdot \left\lvert H \right\rvert }. \]

Corrected. An earlier draft of this lemma claimed \(\delta \bigl(\left\{ \mathfrak {p}: \sigma _\mathfrak {p}= \sigma \right\} \bigr) = 1/(\left\lvert G \right\rvert \cdot \left\lvert H \right\rvert )\) — that is mathematically wrong (the set \(\left\{ \sigma _\mathfrak {p}= \sigma \right\} \) has density \(1/\left\lvert G \right\rvert \), not \(1/(\left\lvert G \right\rvert \cdot \left\lvert H \right\rvert )\)). The actual per-\(m\) step that feeds into the proof of \(\delta (\sigma _\mathfrak {p}= \sigma ) = 1/\left\lvert G \right\rvert \) is the \(\liminf \) lower bound above (Sharifi p. 144).

Proof ▶

By lemma 1.1, the fixed field \(F = L(\zeta _m)^{\left\langle (\sigma ,\tau ) \right\rangle }\) satisfies \(F(\zeta _m) = L(\zeta _m)\), so the extension \(L(\zeta _m)/F\) is cyclotomic. Apply the cyclotomic case theorem 1.8 to \(L(\zeta _m)/F\) to obtain \(\delta _F = 1/\left\lvert \left\langle (\sigma ,\tau ) \right\rangle \right\rvert \). The conjugacy-class reduction (theorem 1.3 below, Step 1’s counting) lifts this to a \(K\)-density of \(1/(\left\lvert G \right\rvert \cdot \left\lvert H \right\rvert )\).

Lemma 1.3
#

Lean declarations

  • Chebotarev.H_n_over_H_lower_bound_via_prime_factorisation

Let \(n = p_1^{k_1}\cdots p_r^{k_r}\) with \(p_i\) distinct primes, \(k_i\ge 1\). For an integer \(m \ge 1\) with \(m \equiv 1 \pmod{n^j}\), setting \(j_i = v_{p_i}(m-1) \ge j\) and \(H_n = \left\{ \tau \in (\mathbb {Z}/m\mathbb {Z})^\times \; \middle |\; n\mid \mathrm{ord}(\tau ) \right\} \),

\[ \frac{\left\lvert H_n \right\rvert }{\left\lvert (\mathbb {Z}/m\mathbb {Z})^\times \right\rvert } \; =\; \prod _{i=1}^r\! \biggl(1-\frac{p_i^{k_i-1}}{p_i^{j_i k_i}}\biggr) \; \ge \; \prod _{i=1}^r\! \biggl(1-\frac{1}{p_i^{(j-1)k_i+1}}\biggr). \]
Proof ▶

Direct combinatorial computation on \((\mathbb {Z}/m\mathbb {Z})^\times \) using CRT and the prime-power factorisation of \(n\) (Sharifi p. 144).

As \(k \to \infty \), \(\left\lvert H_n \right\rvert /\left\lvert (\mathbb {Z}/n^k\mathbb {Z})^\times \right\rvert \to 1\).

Limit of the product formula lemma 1.3 as \(j \to \infty \): each factor tends to \(1\).

Lean declarations

  • Chebotarev.ratioSum_frobeniusFibres_tendsto_one

As \(s\downarrow 1\), the sum over \(\sigma \in G\) of the density ratios of the fibres \(\left\{ \mathfrak {p} \; \middle |\; \sigma _\mathfrak {p}=\sigma \right\} \) tends to \(1\).

Proof ▶

The fibres partition the unramified primes; the ramified primes are finite (lemma 1.11) hence contribute density \(0\), so the partial sums of the fibre ratios equal the ratio for the unramified primes, which \(\to 1\).

Lemma 1.7
#

Lean declarations

  • Chebotarev.tendsto_inv_card_of_liminf_ge_of_sum_tendsto_one

Let \(g_i\) (\(i\) in a finite index set of size \(N\)) be real functions with \(\liminf _{s\downarrow 1} g_i \ge 1/N\) for each \(i\), and \(\sum _i g_i(s) \to 1\) as \(s\downarrow 1\). Then \(g_i(s) \to 1/N\) for every \(i\).

Proof ▶

Pure real analysis: the lower bounds and the sum-limit pin every \(g_i\) to \(1/N\) by a \(\liminf \)/\(\limsup \) pigeonhole.

Lean declarations

  • Chebotarev.chebotarev_abelian

For a finite abelian Galois extension \(L/K\) of number fields with \(G=\mathrm{Gal}(L/K)\) and every \(\sigma \in G\),

\[ \delta \bigl(\left\{ \mathfrak {p}\subset \mathcal{O}_K \; \middle |\; \sigma _\mathfrak {p}= \sigma \right\} \bigr) \; =\; \frac{1}{\left\lvert G \right\rvert }. \]
Proof ▶

The \(\left\lvert G \right\rvert \) fibres each have \(\liminf \ge 1/\left\lvert G \right\rvert \) (lemma 1.5) and their density ratios sum to \(1\) (lemma 1.6); the pigeonhole glue lemma 1.7 forces each fibre’s ratio to the limit \(1/\left\lvert G \right\rvert \).
