1 Decomposition group, inertia, Frobenius

Let \(L/K\) be a finite Galois extension of number fields with \(G=\mathrm{Gal}(L/K)\). The Galois group acts on ideals of \(\mathcal{O}_L\) (via the canonical \(G\)-action restricted to \(\mathcal{O}_L\) and extended multiplicatively), and in particular permutes the primes lying above a given prime \(\mathfrak {p}\) of \(\mathcal{O}_K\).

Definition 1.1
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • Chebotarev.UnramifiedIn

A nonzero prime ideal \(\mathfrak {p}\) of \(\mathcal{O}_K\) is unramified in \(L\) if every prime \(\mathfrak {P}\) of \(\mathcal{O}_L\) lying over \(\mathfrak {p}\) has ramification index \(1\), i.e. \(\mathfrak {p}\mathcal{O}_L\) factors into distinct primes.

Definition 1.2
✓

  • Chebotarev.decompositionGroup

For a prime \(\mathfrak {P}\) of \(\mathcal{O}_L\), the decomposition group at \(\mathfrak {P}\) is

\[ D_\mathfrak {P}\; =\; \left\{ \sigma \in G : \sigma (\mathfrak {P})=\mathfrak {P} \right\} \; =\; \mathrm{Stab}_G(\mathfrak {P}). \]
Definition 1.3
✓
#

Uses

  • Chebotarev.inertiaGroup

The inertia group at \(\mathfrak {P}\) is

\[ I_\mathfrak {P}\; =\; \left\{ \sigma \in G \; \middle |\; \forall x\in \mathcal{O}_L,\; \sigma (x)\equiv x\pmod\mathfrak {P} \right\} . \]

This is the generic ideal-inertia subgroup Ideal.inertia from mathlib, instantiated at the natural action of \(G\) on \(\mathcal{O}_L\); acting trivially mod \(\mathfrak {P}\) forces stabilising \(\mathfrak {P}\), so \(I_\mathfrak {P}\) sits inside \(D_\mathfrak {P}\), and is the kernel of the action of \(D_\mathfrak {P}\) on the residue field \(\mathcal{O}_L/\mathfrak {P}\).

Lemma 1.4
#

Uses

  • Definition 1.3
  • Definition 1.1

Lean declarations

  • Chebotarev.inertiaGroup_trivial_of_unramified

If \(\mathfrak {P}\) is unramified over \(\mathfrak {p}=\mathfrak {P}\cap \mathcal{O}_K\), then \(I_\mathfrak {P}=\{ 1\} \).

Proof ▶

Mathlib’s Ideal.ramificationIdx_eq_one_of_isUnramifiedAt gives the forward equivalence between unramifiedness and ramification index \(1\); combined with \(\left\lvert I_\mathfrak {P} \right\rvert =e(\mathfrak {P}\mid \mathfrak {p})\) this yields the claim.

When \(\mathfrak {P}\) is unramified, the surjection \(D_\mathfrak {P}\to \mathrm{Gal}((\mathcal{O}_L/\mathfrak {P})/(\mathcal{O}_K/\mathfrak {p}))\) (mathlib’s IsFractionRing.stabilizerHom_surjective) has trivial kernel lemma 1.4, so it is an isomorphism. The residue Galois group of a finite-field extension is canonically cyclic, generated by the absolute Frobenius \(x\mapsto x^{N\mathfrak {p}}\) (mathlib’s FiniteField.frobeniusAlgEquivOfAlgebraic). Hence \(D_\mathfrak {P}\) has a distinguished element.

Lemma 1.5

Lean declarations

  • Chebotarev.exists_unique_frobeniusAt_of_inertia_trivial

Let \(\mathfrak {P}\) be a nonzero prime of \(\mathcal{O}_L\) with trivial inertia group \(I_\mathfrak {P}= \{ 1\} \). There is a unique \(\sigma \in D_\mathfrak {P}\) such that \(\sigma (x)\equiv x^{N\mathfrak {p}}\pmod\mathfrak {P}\) for every \(x\in \mathcal{O}_L\).

Proof ▶

With \(I_\mathfrak {P}\) trivial, the surjection \(D_\mathfrak {P}\to \mathrm{Gal}(\kappa (\mathfrak {P})/\kappa (\mathfrak {p}))\) is an isomorphism, and the residue Galois group is cyclic with distinguished generator the absolute Frobenius; pull it back.

For an unramified nonzero prime \(\mathfrak {P}\) of \(\mathcal{O}_L\), the Frobenius automorphism \(\mathrm{Frob}_\mathfrak {P}\in G\) is the unique element of \(D_\mathfrak {P}\) characterised by \(\mathrm{Frob}_\mathfrak {P}(x)\equiv x^{N\mathfrak {p}}\pmod\mathfrak {P}\) for all \(x\in \mathcal{O}_L\).

Definition 1.8
#

Uses

  • Definition 1.7

Lean declarations

  • Chebotarev.frobeniusClass

For a nonzero prime \(\mathfrak {p}\) of \(\mathcal{O}_K\) unramified in \(L\), the Frobenius conjugacy class is

\[ \sigma _\mathfrak {p}\; =\; \left\{ \mathrm{Frob}_\mathfrak {P}: \mathfrak {P}\mid \mathfrak {p} \right\} \; \in \; \mathrm{Conj}(G), \]

the conjugacy class in \(G\) containing \(\mathrm{Frob}_\mathfrak {P}\) for any (equivalently every) prime \(\mathfrak {P}\) of \(\mathcal{O}_L\) above \(\mathfrak {p}\). The class is independent of the choice of \(\mathfrak {P}\).

Lemma 1.9

  • Chebotarev.exists_frobeniusClass

For a nonzero prime \(\mathfrak {p}\) of \(\mathcal{O}_K\) unramified in \(L\), there exists a conjugacy class \(C \subseteq \mathrm{Gal}(L/K)\) such that for every prime \(\mathfrak {P}\) of \(\mathcal{O}_L\) above \(\mathfrak {p}\), \(C = [\mathrm{Frob}_\mathfrak {P}]\). (Equivalently, the Frobenius elements above \(\mathfrak {p}\) are all conjugate.)

Lemma 1.11
#

Uses

  • Definition 1.1

Lean declarations

  • Chebotarev.finite_ramifiedIn

Only finitely many nonzero primes of \(\mathcal{O}_K\) ramify in \(L\).

Proof ▶

The primes ramifying in \(L\) are exactly the divisors of the relative different ideal \(\mathfrak {d}_{L/K}\subseteq \mathcal{O}_L\), which has a finite prime factorisation in the Dedekind domain \(\mathcal{O}_L\).
