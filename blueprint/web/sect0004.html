1 Chebotarev: cyclotomic case

For a cyclotomic extension \(L = K(\zeta _m)\), Chebotarev’s theorem reduces directly to Dirichlet’s argument. Source: Sharifi 7.2.1 (pp. 142–143).

Lemma 1.1

Lean declarations

  • Chebotarev.cyclotomic_frobenius_acts_as_norm_power

Let \(L = K(\zeta _m)\), \(\mathfrak {p}\) a nonzero prime of \(\mathcal{O}_K\) unramified in \(L\), and \(\mathfrak {P}\) a prime of \(\mathcal{O}_L\) above \(\mathfrak {p}\). Then for every primitive \(m\)-th root of unity \(\zeta \in L\),

\[ \mathrm{Frob}_\mathfrak {P}(\zeta ) \; =\; \zeta ^{N\mathfrak {p}}. \]
Proof ▶

By the defining property of \(\mathrm{Frob}_\mathfrak {P}\) (acts as \(N\mathfrak {p}\)-power on \(\mathcal{O}_L/\mathfrak {P}\)) combined with the fact that \(\zeta _m\) lifts isomorphically from the residue field for \(\mathfrak {p}\nmid m\).

For every character \(\chi \) of \(G\), the partial sum \(\sum _\mathfrak {p}\chi (\mathrm{Frob}_\mathfrak {p}) N\mathfrak {p}^{-s}\) over \(\mathfrak {p}\) unramified in \(L\) is bounded by \(C\log (1/(s-1)) + C\) for some constant \(C\), in a right neighbourhood of \(s = 1\).

Proof ▶

Expand \(\log L(\chi , s)\) via the Euler product lemma 1.2; the higher-power tail is bounded analogously to lemma 1.2.

Let \(L = K(\zeta _m)\), \(\sigma \in G\), and \(\mathfrak {p}\) a nonzero prime of \(\mathcal{O}_K\) unramified in \(L\) with \(\sigma _\mathfrak {p}\) the conjugacy class of \(\sigma \). Then

\[ \sum _{\chi \in \widehat G} \chi (\sigma )\chi (\mathrm{Frob}_\mathfrak {p})^{-1} \; =\; \left\lvert G \right\rvert . \]

Lean declarations

  • Chebotarev.character_orthogonality_cyclotomic_ne

Let \(L = K(\zeta _m)\), \(\sigma \in G\), and \(\mathfrak {p}\) a nonzero prime of \(\mathcal{O}_K\) unramified in \(L\) whose Frobenius class is not the conjugacy class of \(\sigma \). Then

\[ \sum _{\chi \in \widehat G} \chi (\sigma )\chi (\mathrm{Frob}_\mathfrak {p})^{-1} \; =\; 0. \]
Proof ▶

Standard finite-group character orthogonality for the dual of \(G\) (Sharifi 7.2.1 step at p. 142). Under the isomorphism \(G\cong (\mathbb {Z}/m\mathbb {Z})^\times \) from lemma 1.1, \(\chi (\mathrm{Frob}_\mathfrak {p})\) depends only on \(N\mathfrak {p}\bmod m\), and the sum reduces to the orthogonality relation on the cyclic group \((\mathbb {Z}/m\mathbb {Z})^\times \).

  • Chebotarev.primeIdealZetaSum_frobeniusFibre_asymp

Let \(L = K(\zeta _m)\) and \(\sigma \in G\). The Frobenius-fibre prime sum is asymptotic to \((1/\left\lvert G \right\rvert )\log (1/(s-1))\):

\[ \lim _{s\downarrow 1} \frac{\sum _{\mathfrak {p}:\, \sigma _\mathfrak {p}= \sigma } N\mathfrak {p}^{-s}}{\log (1/(s-1))} \; =\; \frac{1}{\left\lvert G \right\rvert }. \]
Proof ▶

Multiply \(\log L(\chi , s)\) by \(\chi (\sigma )^{-1}\) and sum over \(\chi \): the orthogonality relations (??) pick out the primes with Frobenius \(\sigma \), giving \(\sum _\chi \chi (\sigma )^{-1}\log L(\chi , s) \sim \left\lvert G \right\rvert \sum _{\sigma _\mathfrak {p}=\sigma } N\mathfrak {p}^{-s}\); on the other hand it \(\sim \log \zeta _K(s) \sim \log (1/(s-1))\) (only \(\chi =1\) contributes a pole, by lemma 1.6). Divide.

Lemma 1.6
✓

Lean declarations

  • Chebotarev.tendsto_ratio_of_log_asymp_numerator

If \(\mathrm{num}(s)/\log (1/(s-1)) \to c\) and \(\mathrm{den}(s)/\log (1/(s-1)) \to 1\) as \(s\downarrow 1\), then \(\mathrm{num}(s)/\mathrm{den}(s) \to c\).

Proof ▶

Write \(\mathrm{num}/\mathrm{den} = (\mathrm{num}/L)/(\mathrm{den}/L)\) with \(L = \log (1/(s-1))\), which is positive (hence nonzero) for \(s\in (1,2)\) since \(1/(s-1) {\gt} 1\); apply the quotient rule for limits and cancel \(L\).

Let \(L = K(\zeta _m)\) and \(\sigma \in G\). Then

\[ \lim _{s\downarrow 1} \frac{\sum _{\mathfrak {p}:\, \sigma _\mathfrak {p}= \sigma } N\mathfrak {p}^{-s}}{\sum _\mathfrak {p}N\mathfrak {p}^{-s}} \; =\; \frac{1}{\left\lvert G \right\rvert }. \]

The numerator asymptotic lemma 1.5 (\(\mathrm{num}\sim (1/\left\lvert G \right\rvert )\log (1/(s-1))\)) and the denominator asymptotic lemma 1.10 (\(\mathrm{den}\sim \log (1/(s-1))\)) feed the ratio glue lemma 1.6.
