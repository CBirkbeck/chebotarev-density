1 Dirichlet density of a set of prime ideals

Throughout, \(K\) denotes a number field, \(\mathcal{O}_K\) its ring of integers, and \(\mathfrak {p}\) ranges over nonzero prime ideals of \(\mathcal{O}_K\) with absolute norm \(N\mathfrak {p}=[\mathcal{O}_K:\mathfrak {p}]\).

Definition 1.1
✓
#

Lean declarations

  • Chebotarev.HasDirichletDensity

A set \(S\) of nonzero prime ideals of \(\mathcal{O}_K\) has Dirichlet density \(\delta \in \mathbb {R}\), written \(\delta (S)=\delta \), if

\[ \lim _{s\to 1^+} \frac{\displaystyle \sum _{\mathfrak {p}\in S} N\mathfrak {p}^{-s}}{\displaystyle \sum _{\mathfrak {p}} N\mathfrak {p}^{-s}} = \delta , \]

where the denominator sum runs over all nonzero prime ideals of \(\mathcal{O}_K\).

The denominator is asymptotically \(\log \bigl(1/(s-1)\bigr)\) as \(s\downarrow 1\) (Sharifi Prop. 7.1.12, p. 140); the lemmas below break out the analytic ingredients.

Lemma 1.2
#

Lean declarations

  • Chebotarev.primeIdealZetaHigherTail_bounded

The higher-power tail of the log-Euler-product \(\sum _{\mathfrak {p},k\ge 2}\frac{N\mathfrak {p}^{-2s}}{1-N\mathfrak {p}^{-s}}\) is bounded on a right neighbourhood of \(s=1\).

Lemma 1.3
#

Uses

  • Definition 1.1

Lean declarations

  • Chebotarev.primeIdealZetaSum_le_finrank_smul_riemannPrimeSum

For \(s{\gt}1\) in a right neighbourhood of \(1\),

\[ \sum _{\mathfrak {p}} N\mathfrak {p}^{-s} \; \le \; [K:\mathbb {Q}]\sum _{p\text{ rational prime}} p^{-s}. \]
Proof ▶

At most \([K:\mathbb {Q}]\) primes of \(\mathcal{O}_K\) lie above any rational prime \(p\) (the identity \(\sum _{\mathfrak {p}\mid p}e(\mathfrak {p}\mid p)f(\mathfrak {p}\mid p)=[K:\mathbb {Q}]\) in mathlib’s Ideal.sum_ramification_inertia), and the norm of such a prime is at least \(p\), so the bound follows by absolute convergence on \(\operatorname {Re}(s){\gt}1\).

Lemma 1.4
#

Lean declarations

  • Chebotarev.riemannPrimeSum_asymp_log

As \(s\downarrow 1\),

\[ \sum _{p\text{ rational prime}} p^{-s} \sim \log \frac{1}{s-1}. \]
Proof ▶

The case \(K=\mathbb {Q}\) of lemma 1.10 below: combine the Euler product \(\zeta (s)=\prod _p(1-p^{-s})^{-1}\) with the simple pole of \(\zeta \) at \(s=1\).

Euler-product-log identity: \(\bigl|\log \zeta _K(s) - \sum _\mathfrak {p}N\mathfrak {p}^{-s}\bigr| \le C\) on a right neighbourhood of \(1\).

Proof ▶

\(\log \zeta _K(s) = \sum _\mathfrak {p}N\mathfrak {p}^{-s} + \sum _{\mathfrak {p},k\ge 2} N\mathfrak {p}^{-ks}/k\); the tail is bounded by lemma 1.2.

Lemma 1.9
#

Lean declarations

  • Chebotarev.tendsto_ratio_one_of_log_pm_bounded

Let \(f : (1,\infty ) \to \mathbb {R}\). If \(f\) is sandwiched as \(\log \bigl(1/(s-1)\bigr) - C \le f(s) \le \log \bigl(1/(s-1)\bigr) + C'\) on a right neighbourhood of \(1\), then \(f(s)/\log \bigl(1/(s-1)\bigr) \to 1\) as \(s \downarrow 1\).

Proof ▶

Since \(\log \bigl(1/(s-1)\bigr) \to \infty \) as \(s \downarrow 1\), the additive constants \(C, C'\) wash out under division, and the squeeze gives the limit \(1\).

Density API

The following routine API lemmas are used by the proofs of the corollaries in chapter 6.

Lemma 1.11
#

Uses

  • Definition 1.1

Lean declarations

  • Chebotarev.hasDirichletDensity_of_finite

A finite set of prime ideals has Dirichlet density \(0\).

Proof ▶

The numerator \(\sum _{\mathfrak {p}\in S}\mathrm{N}(\mathfrak {p})^{-s}\) is bounded as \(s \to 1^+\) (a finite sum of bounded terms), while the denominator \(\sum _\mathfrak {p}\mathrm{N}(\mathfrak {p})^{-s}\to \infty \) by lemma 1.10; the ratio tends to \(0\).

Lemma 1.12
#

Uses

  • Definition 1.1

Lean declarations

  • Chebotarev.HasDirichletDensity.hasLower

If \(S\) has Dirichlet density \(\delta \), then its lower Dirichlet density is also \(\delta \).

Proof ▶

Convergence of the ratio implies the liminf and limsup coincide with the limit; specifically the liminf equals \(\delta \).
