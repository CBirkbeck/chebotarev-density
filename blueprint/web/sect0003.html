1 Zeta factorisation for an abelian extension

For an abelian Galois extension \(L/K\) of number fields with \(G=\mathrm{Gal}(L/K)\), the Dedekind zeta function \(\zeta _L\) factors as a product of Hecke \(L\)-functions indexed by characters of \(G\). This is the analytic engine of the cyclotomic case of Chebotarev.

  • Chebotarev.exists_artinLSeries_eulerProduct_abelian

For an abelian character \(\chi : G \to \mathbb {C}^\times \), there is a function \(L(\chi , \cdot ): \mathbb {C}\to \mathbb {C}\) such that on \(\operatorname {Re}(s) {\gt} 1\),

\[ L(\chi , s) \; =\; \prod _\mathfrak {p}\bigl(1-\chi (\mathrm{Frob}_\mathfrak {p}) \mathrm{N}(\mathfrak {p})^{-s}\bigr)^{-1}, \]

where the product runs over primes \(\mathfrak {p}\) of \(\mathcal{O}_K\) unramified in \(L\), with the convention \(\chi (\mathfrak {p}) = 0\) on ramified primes.

Proof ▶

Sharifi Prop. 7.1.18 (p. 141). The Euler product converges absolutely on \(\operatorname {Re}(s) {\gt} 1\) by comparison with \(\zeta _K\).

Lemma 1.3
#

Uses

  • Lemma 1.2

Lean declarations

  • Chebotarev.dedekindZeta_local_factor_eq_product_artin_local

For each unramified prime \(\mathfrak {p}\) of \(\mathcal{O}_K\), the local Euler factor of \(\zeta _L\) at \(\mathfrak {p}\) factors as

\[ \prod _{\mathfrak {P}\mid \mathfrak {p}}\bigl(1-\mathrm{N}(\mathfrak {P})^{-s}\bigr)^{-1} \; =\; \prod _\chi \bigl(1-\chi (\mathrm{Frob}_\mathfrak {p})\mathrm{N}(\mathfrak {p})^{-s}\bigr)^{-1}. \]
Proof ▶

Standard cyclic-group character theory applied to the residue Galois group: the characters of \(\mathrm{Gal}((\mathcal{O}_L/\mathfrak {P})/(\mathcal{O}_K/\mathfrak {p}))\) separate elements, and the indicated product over \(\chi \) recovers the local factor (Sharifi 7.1.16 step at \(\mathfrak {p}\)).

Lemma 1.4
#

Lean declarations

  • Chebotarev.character_sum_geometry_of_numbers_bound

For a nontrivial character \(\chi : G \to \mathbb {C}^\times \), there is a constant \(C\) such that

\[ \biggl\lVert \sum _{\mathrm{N}(\mathfrak {a})\le N}\chi (\mathfrak {a})\biggr\rVert \; \le \; C \cdot N^{1 - 1/[K:\mathbb {Q}]} \qquad \text{for all }N\ge 1. \]
Proof ▶

Geometry-of-numbers count of ideals in a norm-bounded region (Sharifi 7.1.19, p. 142, step 1). Per-residue-class count \(C N + O(N^{1-1/[K:\mathbb {Q}]})\); summing over \(\chi (\mathfrak {a}) = \zeta \) for \(\zeta \) a root of unity, the \(C N\) contributions cancel by \(\sum _\zeta \zeta = 0\).

Lemma 1.5
#

Uses

  • Lemma 1.2
  • Lemma 1.4

Lean declarations

  • Chebotarev.artinLSeries_analytic_extension

For every nontrivial character \(\chi : G \to \mathbb {C}^\times \), the Dirichlet series \(L(\chi , s) = \sum _{\mathfrak {a}} \chi (\mathfrak {a})\mathrm{N}(\mathfrak {a})^{-s}\) extends to a function analytic on \(\operatorname {Re}(s) {\gt} 1 - 1/[K:\mathbb {Q}]\), agreeing with the Euler product on \(\operatorname {Re}(s) {\gt} 1\). In particular, \(L(\chi , 1)\) is well-defined.

Proof ▶

Combine lemma 1.4 (Sharifi 7.1.19 step 1a) with Sharifi Lemma 7.1.5 (p. 138, a generic Dirichlet-series convergence criterion from a polynomial bound on partial sums): given \(\bigl|\sum _{n\le N}a_n\bigr|\le CN^{u}\), the Dirichlet series \(\sum _n a_n n^{-s}\) converges absolutely and uniformly on every compact subset of \(\operatorname {Re}(s) {\gt} u\). Applied to \(a_{\mathfrak {a}} = \chi (\mathfrak {a})\) with \(u = 1 - 1/[K:\mathbb {Q}]\), the series defines an analytic function on \(\operatorname {Re}(s) {\gt} 1 - 1/[K:\mathbb {Q}]\).

Lemma 1.6
#

Uses

  • Lemma 1.5

Lean declarations

  • Chebotarev.artinLSeries_one_ne_zero

For every nontrivial character \(\chi : G \to \mathbb {C}^\times \), \(L(\chi , 1) \ne 0\). (Here \(L(\chi , 1)\) refers to the analytic extension of lemma 1.5.)

Proof ▶

Sharifi 7.1.19 step 2 (p. 142). Write \(\log \zeta _L(t) = \sum _\chi \log L(\chi , t)\) for \(t {\gt} 1\). Up to a bounded function as \(t \to 1^+\), the right side has absolute value at least \(\bigl(1 - \sum _\chi m_\chi \bigr)\log (t-1)^{-1}\), where \(m_\chi \) is the order of vanishing of \(L(\chi , s)\) at \(s = 1\). If any \(m_\chi \ge 1\), this is at most \(0\cdot \log (t-1)^{-1}\), contradicting \(\log \zeta _L(s) \sim \log (s-1)^{-1}\).

  • Chebotarev.exists_dedekindZeta_factorisation

Let \(L/K\) be a finite abelian Galois extension of number fields. There is a family of functions \(\left\{ L(\chi , \cdot ) : \chi \in \widehat{G} \right\} \), each holomorphic on \(\operatorname {Re}(s) {\gt} 1\), satisfying:

  1. \(\displaystyle \zeta _L(s) \; =\; \prod _{\chi \in \widehat{G}} L(\chi , s)\) for \(\operatorname {Re}(s) {\gt} 1\);

  2. \(L(\mathbf{1}, s) = \zeta _K(s)\);

  3. \(L(\chi , 1) \ne 0\) for every \(\chi \ne \mathbf{1}\).

Proof ▶

Compose lemma 1.3 prime by prime, using the absolute convergence of the global Euler product; combine with the non-vanishing lemma 1.6.
