## Sources

The Frobenius-symbol-as-conjugacy-class facts are SL pp. 13–14:
> (b) "if ψ, ψ′ are two places over p, then ψ′ = ψ ∘ τ for some τ ∈ G"
> (p. 14) "Frob_{ψ∘τ} = τ⁻¹ ∘ Frob_ψ ∘ τ; that is, if ψ varies over the places
> over a fixed prime p, then Frob_ψ ranges over a conjugacy class in G … it is
> well-defined only up to conjugacy, and it is called the Frobenius substitution of p."

Places over `p` ↔ primes `𝔓 | 𝔭`; `ψ ↦ ψ ∘ τ` ↔ the Galois action `𝔓 ↦ σ • 𝔓`.

## R: `exists_frobeniusClass` — DONE (composed sorry-free this pass)

`Frobenius.lean`. The Frobenius conjugacy class of an unramified `𝔭` is well-defined:
there is a `C : ConjClasses Gal(L/K)` equal to `mk (frobeniusAt 𝔓)` for *every* prime
`𝔓 | 𝔭`. SL p. 14: the Frobenius substitution is the conjugacy class swept out as `𝔓`
ranges over primes above `𝔭`.

Composition (`exists_frobeniusClass := by …`, sorry-free):
- pick `𝔓₀ | 𝔭` via **L-new** `exists_prime_liesOver`;
- set `C := mk (frobeniusAt 𝔓₀)`;
- for any `𝔓 | 𝔭`, `mk (frobeniusAt 𝔓₀) = mk (frobeniusAt 𝔓)` by
  `ConjClasses.mk_eq_mk_iff_isConj` + **L1** `frobeniusAt_isConj_of_liesOver`.

### L-new — `exists_prime_liesOver` (leaf, PROVEN from mathlib)
A nonzero prime `𝔭` of `𝓞 K` has a prime `𝔓 | 𝔭` of `𝓞 L`, and any such `𝔓 ≠ ⊥`.
- Discharge (verified, `lean_run_code` + `lake build`):
  `Ideal.exists_ideal_over_prime_of_isIntegral_of_isDomain` (going-up, integral
  extension `𝓞 K ⊆ 𝓞 L`) gives the prime with `comap = 𝔭`; nonzero from
  `RingHom.ker_eq_comap_bot` + injectivity of `algebraMap (𝓞 K) (𝓞 L)`
  (`FaithfulSMul.algebraMap_injective`).
- Attacks: [1] counterexample — going-up is unconditional for integral extensions,
  no contradiction. [2] edge `𝔭 = ⊥` excluded by hypothesis; `𝔓 = ⊥` ⇒ `𝔭 = ⊥` via
  the kernel argument, refuted. [3] hypotheses: `hpr` needed (going-up wants a prime),
  `hnz` needed for the nonzero conclusion. Survived.

`Σ_{𝔭∈S} N𝔭⁻ˢ` is bounded (finitely many terms, each `≤ 1`) while the denominator

- **`primeIdealZetaSum_univ_tendsto_atTop`** (PROVEN): `Σ_univ N𝔭⁻ˢ → +∞` as `s↓1`.

  (`Filter.Tendsto.const_mul_atTop`), so `tendsto_atTop_mono'` closes it.

- parent body: `tendsto_of_tendsto_of_tendsto_of_le_of_le'` squeeze between `0` and

## R: `card_primesAbove_mul_orderOf_eq` (Main.lean) — DONE (decomposed, builds green)

Source: **Sharifi 7.2.2 Step 1, p. 143** (verbatim, Main.lean docstring):
> "As `P` is by definition inert in `L`, there are exactly `|G|/f` primes of `L` over
> `P ∩ K`."

Orbit–stabilizer count over `K`: `#{𝔓 of 𝓞 L above 𝔭} · f = |G|`, where the residue degree
`f = [κ(𝔓):κ(𝔭)] = ord σ` (`D_𝔓` is cyclic of order `f` generated by `Frob_𝔓 ∈ C = [σ]`).

**Plain-English proof.** `Gal(L/K)` acts transitively on the primes above `𝔭` with
stabiliser `D_𝔓`; orbit–stabilizer gives `#{primes above 𝔭}·|D_𝔓| = |G|`. Unramified ⟹
inertia trivial ⟹ `|D_𝔓| = f`. `Frob_𝔓` generates `D_𝔓` so `ord(Frob_𝔓) = f`; `Frob_𝔓`
conjugate to `σ` ⟹ `ord σ = f`. Substitute.

## R: `count_frobenius_eq_sigma_mul_card_carrier` (Main.lean) — DONE (decomposed, builds green)

Source: **Sharifi 7.2.2 Step 1, p. 143**: "the Frobenius elements of such primes are
distributed evenly among the elements of the conjugacy class `C` of `σ`, exactly `|G|/f|C|`
of these have Frobenius `σ`." i.e. `|{𝔓 above 𝔭 : Frob_𝔓 = σ}| · |C| = |{𝔓 above 𝔭}|`.

**Plain-English proof.** `𝔓 ↦ Frob_𝔓` maps `{above 𝔭}` onto `C` and is `G`-equivariant
(`Frob_{g•𝔓} = g·Frob_𝔓·g⁻¹`, `frobeniusAt_conj_eq`). Transitivity of `G` on `{above 𝔭}`
makes the fibres over conjugate values equipotent; partitioning `{above 𝔭}` over `C` and
collapsing the equal terms gives `|{above 𝔭}| = |C|·|fibre σ|`.

# Decomposition — `logDedekindZeta_sub_primeIdealZetaSum_bounded` (Sharifi 7.1.12) — pass 2026-06-04

Goal (Density.lean): `∃ C, ∀ᶠ s in 𝓝[>] 1, |Real.log (dedekindZeta K s).re − primeIdealZetaSum univ s| ≤ C`,
i.e. `log ζ_K(s) = Σ_𝔭 N𝔭⁻ˢ + O(1)` as `s ↓ 1`.

## Route correction (red flag: a prior pass left the source)
A prior pass routed through the project's **rational-prime** Euler product
(`dedekindZeta_eq_tprod_primePowerSeries`, `ζ_K = ∏'_q [local factor at q]`) and was forced
into the "local factor identity" `Σ_k mult(qᵏ)(qᵏ)⁻ˢ = ∏_{𝔭∣q}(1−N𝔭⁻ˢ)⁻¹` — an ideal↔
prime-exponent bijection mathlib lacks, ≈400 LOC. Per the `/develop` red-flag rule (a leaf
needing substantial mathlib-absent infrastructure ⇒ the route left the source), I re-read
Sharifi 7.1.12's proof (p.140). **Sharifi never groups by rational primes.** He builds the
prime-ideal Euler product directly by finite-set comparison (Prop 7.1.9's method, p.139,
applied to ideals). So the keystone is the *direct* prime-ideal Euler product, provable from
mathlib's Dedekind-domain ideal-factorisation API — the rational-prime bridge is dropped.

Verbatim (Sharifi 7.1.12 proof, p.140): "we consider the logarithm of the Euler product,
noting that **log ζ_K(s) ∼ Σ_𝔭 N𝔭⁻ˢ** … We can then compare the Euler product over a finite
sum of primes with the partial sum in the Dirichlet series over **ideals divisible only by
those primes** to obtain equality." `∼` = "differ by a function analytic at 1" (Notation
7.1.10, p.140) ⇒ the bounded difference the linchpin asserts.
